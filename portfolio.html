First-author publications

We model donor stars approaching a delayed mass-transfer instability and predict observable pre-instability behaviour. The models suggest a long dimming phase followed by rapid brightening and heating, relevant to luminous red nova progenitors.

Good things come to those who wait (2025)

Open in ADS

A grid of stellar-evolution models maps when post-main-sequence donors undergo stable vs unstable mass transfer. We derive a practical stability criterion tied to the donor’s effective response to mass loss, refining commonly used mass-ratio thresholds.

Coping with loss (2023)

Open in ADS

Binary mergers can produce white dwarfs that appear “single” and systematically bias age estimates. We quantify how large this effect can be and show that merger products can look significantly younger than their true ages.
