Hi, I'm Karel D. Temmink.

I am a PhD candidate in theoretical astrophysics at Radboud University, specializing in the evolution of binary star systems. My research combines computational modelling and data analysis with a strong background in physics and has resulted in eight peer-reviewed publications, including three as first author, with over 900 citations in total and nearly 150 to my first-author work.

Beyond my own research, I am strongly interested in how high-quality science is organized, evaluated, and supported. I enjoy working in multidisciplinary environments where analytical depth, collaboration, and clear decision-making come together, and I am particularly motivated by roles at the interface of research, data, project coordination, and science policy. In a world where communicating science to a broad audience is increasingly important, I focus on making complex ideas accessible without losing depth or rigor, translating intricate concepts into clear, engaging insights that support transparency, collaboration, and impact.
